Meet Your Therapist

Lisa Guest, LPC, LMSW, CPCS

Lisa's path to counseling wasn't a straight line — and that's exactly what makes her good at it. After beginning her career outside the field, she found her calling providing substance use counseling at an all-women's alternative incarceration facility, then committed fully to the work: earning a Bachelor's in Human Services from Mercy College, a Master's in Counseling from Pace University, and a Master's of Social Work from Hunter College.

Over more than twenty years, she has walked alongside clients in hospitals, group homes, criminal justice settings, and faith-based organizations. Today, in her private practice, she brings all of that lived experience to individual and family counseling — meeting every client with warmth, honesty, and zero judgment.

"My goal is to help you uncover your true potential and lead a life that is worth celebrating."

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens in a first session?

Your first session is a relaxed conversation. Lisa will get to know you, understand what brought you in, and work with you to set goals. There's no pressure — just a starting point.

Do you offer telehealth appointments?

Yes. Sessions are available via telehealth, so you can get support wherever you are.

What is solution-focused therapy?

Rather than dwelling only on the past, solution-focused therapy emphasizes your strengths and builds practical, forward-looking strategies — so you leave sessions with tools you can use right away.

Is everything I share confidential?

Yes. Everything discussed in session is confidential within the limits of the law, and this website's appointment form is only visible to the practice owner.

What are your hours?

Appointments are available Tuesday through Saturday, by appointment only. The practice is closed Sunday and Monday.
